What is Flappy Bird?

Built in a weekend.

A solo developer made a game in 2–3 days. It hit #1 in 53 countries, reportedly earned ~$50,000 a day — then he deleted it at the peak.

↓ scroll for the verified story
The whole game in one verb

One tap. That's it.

Flappy Bird is a one-button arcade survival game. Keep a bird in the air by tapping to flap it through gaps between green pipes. A single mistake ends the run.

👆

Input

A single tap — the only control there is.

🎯

Goal

Fly as far as you can without hitting anything.

➕

Score

+1 point for every pair of pipes you clear.

💥

Fail

Touch a pipe, the ground or ceiling → game over.

Rise and disappearance

The timeline

May 24, 2013
Released on the iOS App Store by Dong Nguyen (studio: dotGears). Working title: “Flap Flap.”
Early 2014
Goes viral months after launch — reaches #1 free in 53 countries.
Feb 10, 2014
Nguyen pulls it from both stores at the height of its popularity — saying it had become too addictive. Not a legal issue.
The psychology

Why you can't put it down

Instant death plus instant restart creates the shortest possible failure-to-retry loop. No menus, no waiting — you fail and you're already playing again.

Verified vs. refuted

What survived fact-checking

Every claim was run through 3-vote adversarial verification. These got confirmed — and these popular "facts" got killed.

✓ Confirmed

  • Solo dev Dong Nguyen / dotGears, built in 2–3 days
  • iOS May 24, 2013 · #1 in 53 countries
  • Removed Feb 10, 2014, at peak popularity
  • ~$50K/day from ads (self-reported), 50M+ downloads
  • Free, ad-supported, no paid version
  • Removed for addictiveness — not a legal threat

✕ Refuted myths

  • "90 million downloads"
  • "Hit #1 on Jan 17 / removal tweet Feb 9"
  • "The original physics constants are known"

The original 2013 physics (gravity, flap force, pipe speed) were never published — so our build tunes the feel by hand.

How we rebuilt it

React for the menu. Canvas for the pixels.

The entire game is one loop running ~60 times a second. Here it is:

// every frame (~60fps):
velocity += gravity        // bird always falls
on tap: velocity = flap   // tap = upward kick
bird.y  += velocity
move pipes left
if bird clears a pipe pair: score += 1
if bird hits pipe / ground / ceiling: game over

⌁ Because the original constants are unknown, GRAVITY, FLAP, PIPE_GAP and PIPE_SPEED sit at the top of the file — tuned by feel. Real game dev is iteration, not magic numbers.
